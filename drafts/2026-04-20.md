### Post
Brian Scanlan at Intercom shipped a CLI this week that lets an agent sign up for Intercom, verify its own email by reading Gmail, and finish installation without a human touching the screen.

His framing is the part worth reading twice. If you do not build an agent-friendly path, your customers' agents will brute-force your website, burn tokens, get frustrated, and eventually press escape and build the workaround themselves. The switching cost is one keystroke. Your conversion funnel is invisible, and your drop-off point is "forget it, let's do this a different way."

The evidence is stacking fast. Shopify's AI Toolkit ships write access to 5.6 million live stores through MCP, with no draft step in the middle. Intercom now authors 90 percent of its pull requests with Claude Code. X cut the cost of reading owned data through its API by 5x this week and explicitly invited developers to build agents on top. There is even a site, isitagentready.com, that scans your domain for robots.txt, sitemap, MCP endpoints, Agent Skills, and OAuth readiness, and grades you.

The misconception is treating "agent-friendly" as adding a chat widget. Real agent-readiness means your signup, your docs, your API, your auth, and your install path all work for a headless client that cannot see your hero image and will not sit through your launch video.

If an agent cannot sign up for your product in under 30 seconds without human help, you are not losing the deal to a competitor. You are losing it to the model deciding your category is not worth the context window.

Builders: what is the first thing an agent would fail at on your product today?
