### Post
Every startup is now every other startup. The cost to go multiproduct just hit zero, and nobody's talking about what that breaks.

Ed Sim calls it the Law of Agent Cannibalism. When agents can ship a new feature category overnight, every company creeps into every adjacent market. Lovable went from app builder to data science, marketing, and decks in the same sprint. Security vendors are expanding into adjacent categories faster than CISOs can evaluate them. The capital pressure forces it. Raise at a massive valuation and you must go multiproduct to justify it.

The economics look obvious. Why sign a 3-year contract when an adjacent vendor is building into your vendor's territory with a weekend of agent-assisted development?

But here's what most people miss. The same agent-powered speed that lets you clone features is silently degrading the one thing that would make your version different: your team's ability to think.

New research out of UC Berkeley found that students addicted to their phones are 30% more likely to become cognitively dependent on AI. When researchers asked how many of their peers had stopped actively participating in their education, the estimate was 50%. At the number one public university in the world. Gen Z will make up 30% of the knowledge workforce within four years. The cognitive habits they're forming right now will walk through your door as your next wave of hires.

Operationally, the symptoms are already showing up. Employees shipping vibe-coded prototypes for features nobody asked for. Three-page GPT-generated business cases for $500 decisions. Meetings scheduled to discuss seven AI-generated options no human has actually thought through.

The paradox is structural. Organizations are hiring for AI fluency and then discovering they also need to train for independent thinking. Both are critical requirements, and they pull in opposite directions.

The companies that survive agent cannibalism won't be the ones that ship the most features the fastest. They'll be the ones whose people can still evaluate whether those features are worth shipping at all.
