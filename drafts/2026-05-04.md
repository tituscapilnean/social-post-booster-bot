### Post
Multi-hour agent runs hit the same wall every 1990s database hit. Attention scales O(N²), and the bill arrives.

TheSequence's series this week on the RNN comeback is the cleanest read on why. The argument is uncomfortable for anyone running long agentic workflows on a Transformer-only stack.

Attention is O(N²). The KV cache scales linearly with context length, layers, and batch size, then turns into the binding constraint on concurrency the moment context gets long. NVIDIA Research just published KVTC, a transform-coding pipeline that compresses the KV cache 20 to 40 times for storage. Google Research published TurboQuant, claiming 6x memory and 8x speed gains on long-context inference with no accuracy loss. Both papers are admissions. The default architecture is hitting a physical ceiling, and the industry is patching around it.

New-generation RNNs are matching Transformer perplexity at scale with O(1) inference cost. Constant memory regardless of context length. The original RNN advantage, fixed hidden state, was exactly the property that makes long-running agentic inference economically viable.

The signals layer up. Claude Mythos hit 93.9 percent on SWE-bench Verified by sustaining agentic loops. GLM-5.1 from Z.ai ran 8-hour sessions with 6,000+ tool calls at $1 per million tokens. AgentBrief reported flow-engineering best practices around Sonnet-as-executor with 200K context windows. Every one of those workloads is the worst case for an O(N²) attention bill.

For agent builders, the architectural choice is becoming a P&L choice. If your agent runs for hours and accumulates a long working context, you are pricing your own product into a corner that a recurrent or hybrid architecture might not face.

The bet for the next 18 months is that the harness layer abstracts this away. Routing logic decides whether a step needs full attention or constant-memory recurrence. The MCP gateway is where that decision lives, alongside permissions, rollback, and per-tool budget.

The architecture you ship on stopped being a model decision. It is a billing decision now.
