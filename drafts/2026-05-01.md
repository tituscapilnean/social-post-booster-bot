### Post
74 percent of AI SaaS spend is already token-based. Copilot just admitted the seat-based subscription is dead on June 1.

GitHub Copilot moves to usage-based AI Credits across Pro, Business, and Enterprise on that date. Atlassian and HubSpot announced the same shift the week before. Ramp's data on token-based vs seat-based AI spend was the leading indicator. The Copilot decision is downstream of a much larger constraint. Seat-based pricing stopped being viable for any product where an agent does the actual work.

The math is forced. An agentic coding session can burn 100x the tokens of a flat chat session. Cursor ran a 23 percent negative gross margin last quarter on a flat subscription. Every dollar of revenue cost $1.23 in API spend to Anthropic and OpenAI. Anthropic shipped Mythos at $25 input and $125 output per million tokens, 5x the Opus price. The provider economics already shifted. The application layer just had to admit it.

For founders, the implication is uncomfortable. If your product is a SaaS with agentic features, you are running an unhedged short on inference cost. The flat fee assumed bounded user behavior. Agents do not have bounded behavior. They run loops, retry, fan out. The bill arrives whether or not the user knew what was happening.

The forward question is not whether to switch. It is which unit replaces the seat. Outcome-based pricing punishes the vendor when models hallucinate. Pure usage punishes the user when an agent gets stuck in a loop. Capped pools with overage are where most of the market lands first, then surviving products move to outcome pricing once their evals are good enough to underwrite it.

The companies that win the next eighteen months are the ones whose gateway can meter agent work per customer, per tool, per outcome, with an auditable trail that supports both invoice disputes and product evals.

What does your agent cost per completed task, not per invoice line?
