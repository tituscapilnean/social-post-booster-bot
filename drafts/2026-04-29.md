### Post
For most of the past year, advanced agent builders have been doing a quiet thing. They run a cheap model for the steps, then phone a more expensive one for the plan. Multi-agent papers called it advisor-executor. People shipping in production called it routing.

Anthropic just turned the pattern into an API primitive. The advisor_20260301 tool lets Sonnet or Haiku consult Opus for high-level planning inside a single request. The plan runs 400 to 700 tokens, billed at Opus rates, and steers the executor away from dead-end paths.

The benchmark is the part worth reading carefully. Sonnet plus Opus-as-advisor scored 74.8 percent on SWE-bench Multilingual, 2.7 points above standalone Sonnet, at 11.9 percent lower cost than running Opus through the full trajectory. That delta is a routing story, not a model story. Taste was placed once at the right moment and the rest of the trajectory paid Sonnet rates.

The Leverage's Evan Armstrong made the same argument from the other direction this week. GPT-5.5 at $30 per million output tokens against DeepSeek V4-Pro at $3.48. Tokens are not the unit of value. The unit of value is the harness, the model, and the application stack together. A router that knows when to spend Opus tokens compounds harder than a 2 percent benchmark improvement.

For agent builders, the design question is no longer which model to call. It is where in the call stack a bigger model gets consulted, who pays for the consult, and what gets logged when the executor disagrees with the advisor.

The MCP gateway is exactly the surface where that policy lives. Per-tool budget, advisor checkpoints, rollback hooks, an auditable trail of who decided what.

Show me where in your call stack a human or a bigger model gets invoked. That is the product.
