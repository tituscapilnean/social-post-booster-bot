### Post
$100B over 10 years to Amazon. Up to $40B more to Google. The model layer is not software economics anymore.

Anthropic announced a 10-year, $100B commitment to AWS for up to 5 GW of Trainium capacity. Days later, an additional Google deal worth $10B now plus up to $30B more tied to performance milestones, on top of an existing TPU and Broadcom commitment. Amazon already holds 15 to 20 percent of Anthropic. Google holds 14 percent. The flow is not "raise cash, buy GPUs." It is "trade ownership and revenue share for capacity."

Zoom out one level and the structure gets stranger. OpenAI committed $250B to Azure last fall. Microsoft committed tens of billions to its own data center buildouts financed through the bond market. AWS is shipping Trainium capacity Anthropic prepaid for. Meta and Oracle are doing parallel deals. CoreWeave loaded its balance sheet with debt secured against multi-year GPU contracts from the same labs. The labs commit future revenue to compute providers, the compute providers borrow against those commitments to build capacity, and the banks underwrite the loans on the strength of revenue that has not been earned yet.

A shadow loan economy is forming between AI companies, hyperscalers, and lenders, settling in committed-capacity contracts rather than cash. The collateral is forward demand for inference. The risk lives in any link of the chain that mis-prices that demand.

For founders building on Claude, three things change.

Anthropic will not subsidize you. Mythos at $25 input and $125 output per million tokens is 5x Opus. The Advisor pattern, the Sonnet plus Opus cascade, exists in part because the company cannot afford to let every prompt run on the most capable model. Your cost curve gets shaped by their capacity plan.

Capacity gets allocated to highest-margin customers. Ramp says 74 percent of AI SaaS spend is already usage-based. Flat-fee economics on the consumption side are a temporary subsidy.

The substrate is not neutral. When the company you depend on is paying for compute with its own equity and other people's debt, the contract you signed last quarter is in a renegotiation queue.

Build for the world where inference is priced like power and financed like real estate.
