### Post
Three model labs went vertical into their customers' lanes this month. Cursor saw the math and accepted a $60B call option from SpaceX.

The structure of the Cursor deal is the part to read carefully. SpaceX paid $10B in cash for collaboration plus the option to buy Cursor outright at $60B by year-end. If SpaceX walks, Cursor keeps the $10B. Cursor was hours from closing a $2B round at a $50B valuation when the offer landed. The breakup fee is the actual deal.

In April alone, three labs moved vertically into their customers' lanes. Anthropic deepened its Figma integration. SpaceX-merged xAI took the Cursor option, gaining the highest-quality coding interaction data on earth in exchange for Colossus compute. OpenAI is reportedly building an agent-first phone with Qualcomm and MediaTek. The pattern is consistent. Model providers have decided that selling tokens leaves the majority of value on the table.

The Leverage's Evan Armstrong sized this clearly. Cursor hit $2.7B ARR with 14x year-over-year growth and a 23 percent negative gross margin in January. Every dollar of revenue cost $1.23 in API spend to Anthropic and OpenAI. Margins turned slightly positive only after Cursor shipped Composer, a Kimi 2.5 fine-tune. The unit economics of being a thin layer on top of someone else's model are visibly broken. Cursor had two options. Become a model company, or become someone else's call option.

If you ship vertical SaaS that sits between a foundation model and a knowledge worker, the partnership conversation you had eighteen months ago no longer applies. You are inside a vertical-integration radius. The set of products a frontier lab is willing to absorb expands every quarter.

The defensible surface is the policy layer the lab is structurally bad at owning. Per-customer routing rules. Cross-tool permission graphs. Compliance and rollback at the gateway. The thing a $250B company will not staff a team to maintain across every customer's regulatory boundary.

If a lab shipped your product as a feature next month, what would still be yours?
