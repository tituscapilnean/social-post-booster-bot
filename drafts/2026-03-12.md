# Social Post Draft — 2026-03-12

## Post (X + LinkedIn)

We're spending billions training humans to use AI tools. At the exact same time, those tools are becoming agents that don't need human operators.

Strategyzer put their finger on something real this week. Companies invest in strategy training — Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Design, the whole toolkit. People get energized. Then real work takes over, the tools sit unused, and the same strategic gaps remain.

Their answer: structured application, not just training. Facilitated workshops, guided sprints, methods applied to actual business problems — not one-off events.

The diagnosis is right. The prescription misses the deeper issue.

Training solves the wrong constraint. Humans have to remember to apply what they learned. They have context switches, quarterly plans, meetings about meetings. Coursera's AI training division is a $239M business precisely because the gap between knowing and doing is enormous, and enterprises keep falling into it.

Agents don't fall into that gap. They don't revert to old habits after a sprint. They don't need a quarterly refresher to remember to run the process.

What Strategyzer is building toward — repeatable, structured application of methods across the business at speed — is exactly what an agentic layer does by default. The reason enterprises keep failing at sustained tool adoption isn't culture or capability. The application layer was always supposed to be software, not a training program.

The $239M upskilling economy is racing against its own premise. We're scaling human training on AI workflows at the exact moment those workflows are becoming agent-executable. The people who figure out how to orchestrate agents across their business functions won't need the training. They'll need the judgment about what to automate.

That's a very different skill set than what we're currently teaching.
