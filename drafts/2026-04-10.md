### Post
Lovable hit 400 million in ARR and a 6.6 billion valuation. The same week, Anthropic paused its strongest model and shipped the tool that lets cheaper models call it on demand. The moat moved beneath them. Almost no one noticed.

Mythos went into the lab after proving it could autonomously chain large-scale zero-day discoveries, including a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug. At the same time, Anthropic released the advisor pattern in the Claude API: a Sonnet or Haiku executor runs the agent loop, and only escalates to Opus when the reasoning gets hard. Inside one API request. No external orchestration.

Read the numbers. Sonnet with an Opus advisor is 11.9 percent cheaper per task than Sonnet alone, and 2.7 points better on SWE-bench Multilingual. Haiku with the advisor jumps from 19.7 to 41.2 percent on BrowseComp. That is a small, cheap model getting more than twice as competent because it knows when to ask for help.

The hidden system is this. The frontier just stopped being a model and started being a routing layer. The unit of intelligence is no longer the LLM. It is the orchestration around it. AgentBrief framed it on Friday: the monolithic model era is over.

Lovable, Bolt, Replit clones, and a long tail of wrapper companies are still pricing their products against raw capability. Nate at Substack called the category a moat about a week deep. The routing pattern released this Friday is what threatens them, because every wrapper assumed raw capability was the scarce resource.

The scarce resource is judgment about which model to call, with which context, at which step. Raw capability is just the floor.

That is the layer the next wave of agentic companies will own.
