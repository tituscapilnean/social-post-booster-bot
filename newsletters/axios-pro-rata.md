Dan Primack's Pro Rata focuses on the Musk v. Altman trial as a reputational risk event more than a legal one, plus an unusually large defense-tech funding round.

**Musk v. Altman — what actually matters.** The jury trial ostensibly challenges OpenAI's pivot from nonprofit to for-profit. But Primack's read: the real stakes are reputational and informational, not judicial. "Almost regardless of the jury verdict," what matters is whether either side exposes damaging information about the other. Sam Altman has more at stake: WSJ reported OpenAI "missed its own targets for new users and revenue," and some shareholders have privately questioned whether Altman should lead OpenAI through an IPO. Musk is also prepping a massive SpaceX IPO, but "Wall Street already knows many of his warts and appears to have accepted them." Witness testimony from both Altman and Musk will be the flashpoint. The trial's liability phase runs ~3 weeks with jury advisory opinion, then judge-only remedies phase.

**The macro VC data point.** "Tech investors regularly say that they back people, not projects. Individuals, not ideas." The unprecedented capital concentrations are bets on Altman and Musk as much as on OpenAI and SpaceX. Neither is an actual AI engineer. What happens in the next few weeks "could make or break fortunes."

**True Anomaly raised $650M Series D** at $2.2B valuation (led by Eclipse and Riot Ventures). Autonomous spacecraft and software for US national security. Selected to help develop the Golden Dome missile interceptor program alongside Anduril and defense primes. Investors include Paradigm, Atreides, G Squared, Accel, Menlo Ventures.

**Other venture deals of note.** Ineffable Intelligence: $1.1B seed at $5.1B (Sequoia, Lightspeed, NVIDIA, Google) for RL-based "superlearners." Redpine (Sweden): €6.8M for AI agents accessing non-public data. SkyfireAI (Huntsville): $11M seed for multi-drone autonomy layer.
