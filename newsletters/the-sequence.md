**Thesis: Frontier AI is fragmenting into distinct product forms — the general-purpose reasoning model, the domain specialist, and the workflow-native agent. The race is no longer who has the smartest model on a benchmark; it's who turns intelligence into the most compelling system for actual work.**

**Anthropic's dual push:**
- **Claude Opus 4.7** — refinement of the frontier generalist. Stronger for advanced software work, better instruction following, more reliable in long chains of reasoning.
- **Claude Design** — packages Claude as a collaborator producing visual artifacts (designs, prototypes, slides, one-pagers). Turns design from a prompting exercise into a workflow. Carves a new interface category where reasoning and visual production merge.

**OpenAI parallel story, different angle:**
- **GPT-Rosalind** — rise of the specialist: built specifically for biology, drug discovery, translational medicine. Not a general model stretched into a scientific costume. Signal: next wave of advantage comes from systems deeply adapted to the language, tools, and constraints of high-value domains. Science is one of the first explicit verticalization plays.
- **Codex (new)** — operates a computer, uses tools and apps, remembers preferences, connects to remote environments, handles work over time. Not just a coding assistant; an "agentic operating layer for software and knowledge work." Browses, coordinates, iterates, executes across environments rather than waiting passively.

**Other tech releases:** Gemini on Mac (native macOS app). Chrome Skills (Google integrating AI workflows). Qwen3.6-35B-A3B open-sourced (efficient version of Alibaba's marquee agentic coding model).

**Research highlights:**
- Introspective Diffusion Language Models (I-DLM) — matches AR model quality with improved serving efficiency.
- Experience Replay for LLM RL (FAIR + NYU) — challenges on-policy orthodoxy; well-designed replay buffer cuts inference compute during training.
- Lyra 2.0 (NVIDIA) — explorable generative 3D worlds; per-frame 3D geometry for history retrieval.
- Parcae — stable looped language models with predictable scaling laws.
- ELT (Google DeepMind) — elastic looped transformers for visual generation; Any-Time inference.
- ManyIH (Johns Hopkins) — many-tier instruction hierarchy benchmark. Current frontier models perform poorly resolving conflicts across many privilege levels.
