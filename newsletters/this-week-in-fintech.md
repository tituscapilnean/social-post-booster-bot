**The General Contractor Analogy**
What most fintech operators get wrong about compliance: faced with volume of specialists clamoring for attention (transaction monitoring, California privacy, bank partner audit, Reg E), easy to focus on wrong problem or fall into analysis paralysis. Smartest operators apply the 80/20 rule: know how to spot a doorknob problem and how long they can ignore it.

**Five Compliance Mistakes**

1. **Not understanding the foundation first**: Map regulatory environment (bank partner vs. direct license), funds flow, money transmission triggers, and risk profile before building compliance architecture. Draw matrix of high-impact/high-probability risks. Start with highest risk areas.

2. **Waiting too long to hire in-house compliance**: Hire when building the product. Good compliance leader understands product from day one, reduces regulatory debt (like technical debt, it compounds). Rise of the fractional CCO: experienced compliance leaders working across multiple startups — good fit pre-Series A.

3. **Conflating compliance and legal**: Legal figures out the rules, compliance implements them. Should be some healthy tension: legal minimizes liability by outlining every way to comply; compliance makes those rules work in practice, prioritizing critical requirements first.

4. **Overengineering policies**: A policy is not a textbook. Write it down, be judged on whether you do it. Copy-pasted policies not aligned with actual operations create audit gaps. Shorter, simpler policy accurately describing real processes beats a longer policy not followed. Explicitly warns: "don't use AI to write your policies unless you really know what you're doing."

5. **Understating compliance in strategic conversations**: Well-run compliance program gives leverage in bank partner reviews, enterprise customer due diligence, and acquisition conversations. Aim for 80/20, not perfection — diminishing returns beyond prioritized controls.
