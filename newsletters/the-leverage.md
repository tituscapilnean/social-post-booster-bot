Evan Armstrong's best issue in months — a detailed breakdown of the Cursor/xAI deal and what it reveals about vertical integration as the dominant AI strategy.

**The model vs. harness framing.** GPT-5.5 at $30/M output tokens vs. DeepSeek V4-Pro at $3.48 (effectively 43-107x cheaper with its 80-90% cache-hit discount). Armstrong's argument: tokens are not the unit of value. What matters is the model + harness + application stack together. More and more AI advances are harness- or app-dependent, not pure model magic.

**The Cursor deal mechanics.** SpaceX gets a $60B call option on Cursor, payable in post-IPO stock. If SpaceX walks, Cursor keeps $10B with no dilution. Cursor's financials: $2.7B ARR, 14x YoY, fastest B2B SaaS to $1B ARR ever (beating Slack by two years). Unit economics: negative 23% gross margin in January 2026 — every $1 revenue cost $1.23 in API spend to Anthropic and OpenAI. Margins have since turned slightly positive via "Composer" (a Kimi 2.5 fine-tune with RL on top), but slightly positive may be 0.2%.

**Why xAI needs Cursor.** xAI has compute (post-SpaceX merger combined at $1.25T, largest merger in history) but Grok's coding performance is two tiers below Claude and GPT. Cursor gets cheap compute to train its own frontier model; xAI gets the highest-quality coding interaction data on earth. Coding model improves on coding product data, ships back into coding product, generates more data.

**The SpaceX IPO thesis.** Target: $1.75T valuation, $75B raise. Evan's SOTP math: Starlink at $182B, launch business at $88B, xAI at ~$51B = operating businesses worth $209-321B. The gap to $1.75T is $1.43-1.54T — the "Muskian Multiple." Cursor gives the IPO something tangible: an actual AGI thesis bankers can syndicate, not pure Musk premium.

**The strategic pattern:** Anthropic vertical into design (Figma deal). xAI vertical into coding via Cursor. Model providers have decided selling tokens leaves most value on the table. The blast zone is every workflow a model can do end-to-end. Vertical SaaS sitting between a model and a knowledge worker is now in the path of a vertical-integration play, not a partnership conversation.
